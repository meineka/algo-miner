## Top 5 prioritised findings

### 1. **CRITICAL — no news blackout**
- *Where*: `mt5/Aziz_NQ.mq5` — no news-time gate
- *Why it matters*: NQ rips ± 0.8 % in the first 30 seconds of CPI /
  FOMC / NFP. My rule book says **stop trading 10 minutes before AND
  after high-impact US news**. Without it, your spread filter will
  let trades through that get filled 8 points off price and turn
  every 1R stop into a 4R loss.
- *Fix*: add `Inp_NewsBlackoutTimes` (semicolon-separated `HH:MM,minutes_before,minutes_after`
  list) and a `IsInNewsBlackout()` check in `IsInTradingWindow()`.
  Hard-code US economic-release majors: 13:30 UTC (CPI/PPI/NFP),
  18:00 UTC (FOMC), 14:00 UTC (FOMC minutes).

### 2. **HIGH — Tick-volume on CFDs is a lie**
- *Where*: `mt5/Aziz_NQ.mq5` — `Inp_BreakoutVolMult * MathMax(g_orb_volume_avg, 1.0)`
- *Why it matters*: On a CFD, `tick_volume` is "number of price
  updates", not contracts. It's correlated with activity but
  GoMarkets' price-feed bursts during pre-market can spike tick
  volume 5× without any real order flow. Filter currently passes
  noise as confluence.
- *Fix*: replace tick-volume gate with a **range expansion** filter —
  current bar's range > 1.5 × the 20-bar average range. That's a
  pure-price proxy and broker-agnostic. Keep tick-volume as a
  secondary check, not primary.

### 3. **HIGH — Risk per trade is calculated against EQUITY**
- *Where*: `mt5/Aziz_NQ.mq5::CalculateLotSize` —
  `equity * (Inp_RiskPerTradePct / 100.0)`
- *Why it matters*: After a losing streak `equity < starting_balance`,
  so position size shrinks (good — anti-Martingale) — but after a
  winning streak it inflates. My rule is **1 % of starting balance**
  until you've doubled the account, THEN reset the base. Compounding
  the base too quickly is exactly how my $2 M tariff-war loss in
  April 2025 happened.
- *Fix*: track `g_risk_base_equity` separately from live equity;
  update it weekly (or after a +20 % milestone), not every trade.

### 4. **MEDIUM — No MFE / MAE tracking → blind to where stops should go**
- *Where*: `mt5/Aziz_NQ.mq5` — no max-favourable / max-adverse
  excursion in the journal
- *Why it matters*: After 100 trades you should know "average winning
  trade reached MFE = 2.3R before stopping at +2R" → if it's only
  1.1R, your 2R target is too greedy. If 4R, too tight. Without
  MFE/MAE you're optimising in the dark.
- *Fix*: per-trade, sample `(high − entry) / (entry − sl)` and
  `(entry − low) / (entry − sl)` every tick; print to journal in
  `OnTradeTransaction`. Optional CSV export per backtest run.

### 5. **MEDIUM — Scale-out is two-tier; consider Maróy's 3-tier ladder**
- *Where*: `mt5/Aziz_NQ.mq5` — TP1 50 % @ +1R, TP2 50 % @ +2R
- *Why it matters*: Zarattini & Maróy showed Sharpe goes from 1.33 →
  3.0+ on SPY when ladder exit is 33/33/34 at 1R/2R/3R (with VWAP
  re-cross or 20-EMA break as runner-stop). That's the difference
  between a marginal and an institutional-grade system.
- *Fix*: add `Inp_UseLadder3 = true` → close 33 % at 1R, 33 % at 2R,
  hold 34 % to 3R or VWAP re-cross. Genome knob too in
  `brain/strategy_genome.py`.

## Trade-management improvement for the 2025 tariff war

The April-2025 tariff war was a **3-day liquidity vacuum**: spreads
on SPY/QQQ widened from 1 cent to 10–20 cents, news fired every
30 minutes (Bessent comment → market down 2 %; Trump tweet → market
up 3 %). The EA in its current form would have:
- Got filled at the wide ask, lost 4× the planned R immediately
- Stopped out 3 times → cooldown → never re-entered the recovery
- Missed the dead-cat-bounce setups because spread filter blocked them

**The fix that would have saved me $2 M**: a **volatility gate** that
multiplies the cooldown duration by realized vol. Concretely:

```
if rolling_5d_atr / rolling_60d_atr > 2.0:
   inputs.cooldown_bars     *= 3
   inputs.risk_per_trade    *= 0.5
   inputs.atr_stop_mult     *= 1.5
   inputs.use_vwap_reclaim   = False   # only ORB during chaos
```

Translated to MT5: add `Inp_VolatilityRegimeDetector` running ATR
ratio on the daily timeframe, gate all entry permissions through it.
This is **walk-away mode**: smaller size, wider stops, fewer setups,
no counter-trend.

If I had had this in early-April 2025, my $2 M loss would have been
a $80 K loss and a 4-day pause. That's the entire lesson of the
2025 tariff war in one rule.
