- Took partials at 38.55 → 38.79 → 39.25

### Position sizing rule (verbatim concept)
- *"I trade share sizes of 4 000 to 2 000 — at $37 a share, I can't afford
  a 1- or 2-dollar stop, that'd be $2 000 risk per trade. Not my style."*
- Implication: **position size in shares scales with stop distance, not
  with price** — he keeps risk in dollars roughly constant.

### Risk/reward gate
- *"I want at least 1:1 risk-reward on the ORB entry; ideally 2:1."*
- If the level he's targeting is less than the distance to his stop, he passes.

### Reversal vs pullback (verbatim)
- *"If this is a normal pullback, you come back in like a candle, and yes
  maybe. But this here is a reversal — it's not a good sign for me."*
- The shape of the pullback candle (engulfing vs small) tells you which.

### Afternoon session
- *"I don't trade afternoons — the candles get noisy, levels and momentum
  are hard to find."*
